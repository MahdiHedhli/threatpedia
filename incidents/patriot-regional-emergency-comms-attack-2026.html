CRITICAL INFRASTRUCTURE ATTACK
Patriot Regional Emergency Communications Center in Pepperell, Massachusetts experienced a cyberattack on April 1, 2026 that compromised town and public safety computer systems. 911 services remained operational but non-emergency lines were offline. Federal law enforcement has been notified.

Executive Summary

On April 1, 2026, the Patriot Regional Emergency Communications Center (PRECC) in Pepperell, Massachusetts, suffered a cyberattack that compromised town and public safety computer systems. The attack impacted the dispatch hub serving Pepperell, Ashby, Dunstable, Groton, and other northern Massachusetts municipalities.

While 911 emergency phone lines remained operational throughout the incident, non-emergency dispatch lines went offline. The CodeRED emergency notification system connected to the center was also affected. Police and fire departments continued operations through mutual aid agreements with neighboring communities.

Town Administrator Andrew MacLean engaged the town's insurance provider and coordinated with federal cybersecurity agencies. Law enforcement has been notified and the investigation is ongoing. Attribution remains unknown pending forensic analysis. This incident underscores escalating threats to critical emergency communications infrastructure nationwide.

  • Emergency Notification: CodeRED system compromised, limiting emergency public notification capabilities
  • Mutual Aid: Police and fire departments operated under mutual aid agreements with neighboring jurisdictions to continue service delivery
  • Geographic Scope: Incident affected emergency services for Pepperell, Ashby, Dunstable, Groton, and surrounding northern Massachusetts towns

Investigation Status

  • Federal law enforcement notified and engaged
  • Insurance provider engaged for breach coverage and incident response
  • Forensic investigation ongoing to determine attack methodology and data access scope
  • No attribution currently available pending forensic analysis

Remediation & Recovery

The town of Pepperell and state/federal agencies have initiated remediation and recovery efforts:

Immediate Actions Taken

  • Engaged insurance provider for breach coverage and incident response support
  • Notified federal law enforcement and cybersecurity agencies
  • Isolated compromised systems to prevent further spread
  • Activated mutual aid agreements with neighboring communities
  • Implemented emergency procedures for 911 operations

Recommended Actions

  • Forensic Analysis: Complete comprehensive forensic investigation to understand attack scope and determine attribution
  • Data Breach Assessment: Determine what personal information or sensitive data may have been accessed or exfiltrated
  • System Hardening: Implement network segmentation, access controls, and monitoring for emergency communications systems
  • Backup Verification: Confirm integrity of backups and off-site recovery systems
  • Incident Disclosure: Determine if public notification is required under state or federal breach notification laws
  • Coordination: Engage with Massachusetts state cybersecurity authorities and CISA for guidance on recovery and prevention

Regional Implications

This incident should prompt other emergency communications centers across Massachusetts and the nation to:

  • Conduct vulnerability assessments of CAD systems and emergency dispatch infrastructure
  • Review and test incident response plans for prolonged system outages
  • Verify mutual aid agreements and communication protocols with neighboring jurisdictions
  • Implement enhanced monitoring and threat detection for critical infrastructure networks
  • Consider cybersecurity insurance and incident response retainer agreements
