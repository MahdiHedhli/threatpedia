# Threatpedia

**The Definitive Cyber Threat Encyclopedia**

Threatpedia is a Wikipedia-style, peer-reviewed encyclopedia of cyber history. Every significant incident, attack campaign, and Advanced Persistent Threat group — documented, sourced, framework-mapped, and certified for accuracy by a board of cyber historians, practitioners, and subject matter experts.

> **Status:** Phase 0 — Pre-Launch | Founding interest list open at [threatpedia.wiki](https://threatpedia.wiki)

---

## The Problem

The cyber security community has no single, neutral, comprehensive historical record. Incident knowledge is scattered across vendor reports, news archives, government advisories, and academic papers — rarely structured, rarely linked, and never peer-reviewed to a consistent standard.

Threatpedia exists to fix that.

---

## What We're Building

| Pillar | Description |
|---|---|
| **Incident Encyclopedia** | Every significant cyber event documented with standardized fields — TTPs, timeline, attribution, impact, and source citations |
| **APT Registry** | Current and historical threat groups tracked with profiles, aliases, known campaigns, and state affiliation |
| **Framework Mapping** | Incidents mapped to MITRE ATT&CK, NIST CSF, Cyber Kill Chain, and more |
| **Peer Review & Certification** | Board-reviewed articles earn the Threatpedia Certified stamp — a mark of verified accuracy |
| **Trend Analytics** | Structured data enabling cross-incident research by sector, vector, actor, and time |
| **AI-Augmented Workflow** | AI assists aggregation, pre-screening, and draft generation — humans certify everything |

---

## Core Principles

- **Editorial Independence** — no commercial sponsor influences content or certification decisions
- **Community Ownership** — governed by practitioners, historians, and journalists
- **Structured by Default** — every article captures standardized fields enabling data analysis
- **Human Verified** — AI assists, but all certified content passes peer review
- **Open Access** — core encyclopedia is permanently free
- **Citable** — every article meets academic citation standards
