# Governance

> This document is a placeholder. The Threatpedia governance framework will be finalized by the Founding Board (Phase 1, Q2 2026).

---

## Governance Model

Threatpedia operates as an editorially independent platform governed by a Founding Board of recognized cyber professionals. The model draws from the Wikimedia Foundation structure, adapted for a credentialed expert review process.

---

## Open Items for Founding Board

The following governance questions are on the agenda for the first board meetings:

**Scope & Definition**
- What constitutes a "cyber event" vs. "cyber incident" vs. "cyber campaign"?
- Minimum severity threshold for inclusion
- Historical depth of the record
- Exclusion criteria for unverified or legally contested events

**Data Schema**
- Required vs. optional field finalization
- Event ID and naming convention standards
- Source citation standards
- Handling of classified or sensitive details

**Framework Mapping**
- Mapping methodology and confidence scoring
- Handling of partial or contested mappings
- Priority order for Phase 2 frameworks

**APT Registry**
- Formal definition of an APT for registry inclusion
- Naming conflict resolution across vendor taxonomies
- State sponsorship attribution confidence standards

**Legal & Editorial**
- License finalization (currently CC BY-SA 4.0 — open for board input)
- Policy on naming individual threat actors
- Process for handling legally contested attributions
- Editorial independence policy

## Board Composition (Proposed)

| Role | Function |
|---|---|
| Cyber Historians | Historical scope, timeline validation, narrative accuracy |
| Investigative Journalists | Source quality, editorial standards |
| SMEs by Domain | Technical review across all verticals |
| Legal / Policy Advisors | Responsible disclosure, attribution standards |
| Academic Representatives | Scholarly rigor, citation standards |
| Industry Representatives | CISO-level operational grounding |
